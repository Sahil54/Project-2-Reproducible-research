<p>Storms and other severe weather events have huge impact on public health and economic problems for municipalities and their inhabitants. Some of severe events can cause injuries property damage and even lead to death. This analysis present which types of events are most harmful with respect to population health and which have the greatest economic consequences.</p>
<h1><a id="user-content-data-processing" class="anchor" aria-hidden="true" href="#data-processing"><svg class="octicon octicon-link" viewBox="0 0 16 16" version="1.1" width="16" height="16" aria-hidden="true"><path fill-rule="evenodd" d="M4 9h1v1H4c-1.5 0-3-1.69-3-3.5S2.55 3 4 3h4c1.45 0 3 1.69 3 3.5 0 1.41-.91 2.72-2 3.25V8.59c.58-.45 1-1.27 1-2.09C10 5.22 8.98 4 8 4H4c-.98 0-2 1.22-2 2.5S3 9 4 9zm9-3h-1v1h1c1 0 2 1.22 2 2.5S13.98 12 13 12H9c-.98 0-2-1.22-2-2.5 0-.83.42-1.64 1-2.09V6.25c-1.09.53-2 1.84-2 3.25C6 11.31 7.55 13 9 13h4c1.45 0 3-1.69 3-3.5S14.5 6 13 6z"></path></svg></a>Data Processing</h1>
<p>I'm going to use The U.S. National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration's (NOAA) storm database which tracks characteristics of major storms and weather events in the United States. This dataset comes from the Internet.</p>
<p>Download file from the Internet:</p>

<p>Property damage estimates were entered as actual dollar amounts (the variable PROPDMG). But they were rounded to three significant digits, followed by an alphabetical character signifying the magnitude of the number, i.e., 1.55B for $1,550,000,000. Alphabetical characters used to signify magnitude include ‚ÄúK‚Äù for thousands, ‚ÄúM‚Äù for millions, and ‚ÄúB‚Äù for billions. So I created a new variable PROPDMGEXP2 and assigned conditionally "K" = 1000, "M" = 1000000, "B" = 1000000000, in other cases 1. These variables are multiplied in the next step.</p>
